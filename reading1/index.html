My ansewer

1.
The concept of the homemade web is intricately woven into the historical narrative of web activities, serving as a testament to the evolution of website development over time. By delving into the manual coding of web pages and the maintenance of specific pages by individuals, the homemade web brings forth a nuanced perspective on website creation that goes beyond conventional software-driven approaches. This article sheds light on the pivotal role played by the homemade web in the growth of the World Wide Web, illustrating how the practice of manually coding web pages has contributed to the expansive landscape of the Internet. It underscores the intrinsic value of the homemade web by emphasizing its role in fostering user engagement and providing individuals with the unique opportunity to participate in the creation of web content actively. Furthermore, the article goes beyond mere narration, engaging in conceptual analysis and exercises to delve into the deeper implications of the handmade web. Through this comprehensive exploration, the article aims to articulate the historical context and the enduring significance and potential future implications of the homemade web in the dynamic realm of web development and user interaction.

2.
Carpenter's object of resistance, I believe, is resistance to giant companies, but also to things that require fewer hands from people, can be totally automated, and can be operated by already systematized machinery. The phrase "hand-made" generates the opposite sense of rapidity, uniformity, and automation. Early coding was done in a quiet environment, for the personal use of each user. It is assumed that every user is trying to go back to the first time they created and shaped their online persona through their website. The author contends that this represents a move away from the standardized world and the enterprise-centric online arena, where people can exercise greater autonomy and creativity. From this perspective, "hand-made" is more than just a web construction process; it is also a symbolic phrase for an online experience in which individuals and creativity are respected in a broader context.

3.
Based on my personal experiences, I can think of a few instances when it comes to the physical parts of the web. If I had to give one personal example, it would be the first time I ever learned to code. In the beginning, we approached coding through hands-on exercises rather than using computers to comprehend the structure. If, for example, my mother instructed me to retrieve milk from the refrigerator using a direct human coding method, I would walk in the direction of the refrigerator, stop in front of it, open its door, take out the milk with my hand, close the refrigerator door, turn around, and then walk back to my mom. This kind of understanding of coding at a young age felt like taking a step closer to learning through actual activity. Through this, I learned that coding, whether for websites or anything else, involves careful planning and consideration of what needs to be done.
