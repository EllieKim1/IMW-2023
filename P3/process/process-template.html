P3:Interaction Process

Inspiration, Initial sketches and concepts

Inspiration: I choose to begin this project by looking at one of my favourite pieces of art. I have been assigned with the interaction of scrolling. My initial try was influenced by my favourite painter, Lee Ufan's "from line" piece. I wanted to show how the movements of surfaces and lines are made up of dots. So, as you scroll down, the dots move randomly, and as you scroll more, the dots progressively fade away. But after receiving feedback I made the decision to make something different.
I decided to give the moiré effect an attempt. It was influenced by "Golconda," another one of my favourite paintings by René Magritte, where I caught on from the men's interesting shapes. So I wanted to experiment with a scroll interaction using the moiré effect.

Refined concepts and in-process work

I initially intended to construct many men of comparable size to the design and tried three various layouts, but when I tested it on the actual website, the figures were too little and the movements weren't as evident as I had expected. I made the decision to pursue a different strategy as a result.

Final work

I'm much happier with the second try, myself. Adding a gradient background and having the fish swim downward creates the illusion of them moving underwater. Furthermore, coordinating the scroll movement with the fish's motion improves the engagement dramatically.
